According to a 2022 report, the Philippines has one of the highest income inequality rates in East Asia, with a majority of lower income earners capturing only 14% of the national income and the top 1% contributing 17%.

Overview

As social inequality continues to be a pressing issue globally, the Philippines faces its own challenges in achieving social inclusion and reducing disparities in income distribution. In line with Sustainable Development Goal 10 (SDG 10), which aims to reduce inequality within and among countries, our project investigates income disparity across Philippine regions by analyzing the Gini index.

Objectives

Objective 1

To investigate the differences in income disparity between urban and rural areas in the Philippines by analyzing the Gini indices

Objective 2

To investigate the trends/relationships between income inequality and economic stability

Research Questions

Is there a significant difference in income inequality between urban and rural areas in the Philippines?
