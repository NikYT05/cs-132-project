Overview

As social inequality continues to be a pressing issue globally, the Philippines faces its own challenges in achieving social inclusion and reducing disparities in income distribution. In line with Sustainable Development Goal 10 (SDG 10), which aims to reduce inequality within and among countries, our project investigates the differences in income disparity between urban and rural areas in the Philippines by analyzing the Gini indices.

The Gini index is a summary measure of income inequality within a population. It is derived from the Lorenz curve, which is a graphical representation of income or wealth distribution.

Is there a significant difference in income inequality between urban and rural areas in the Philippines?
